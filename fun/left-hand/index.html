Left-hand handwriting

I once read in a Richard Feynman biography that there was this physicist—Julian Schwinger—who could write with both hands—at the same time! That seems like a bit too much work, but learning to write with my left hand seems doable (and also very cool). Besides being cool (and being a great go-to fun fact), taking notes ambidextrous-ly seems like lots of fun. Whenever one hand gets tired, you can just switch to the other hand!

Day 1: July 20, 2020

I have been periodically practicing my left-hand handwriting for a while now, maybe starting in middle school. As such, I actually started out much worse than this, but I don't have a picture of that. I'm curious to see if there will ever be any visible improvements in my left-hand handwriting, or if I will have to just deal with only being able to write with one hand.

Also, the lyrics are from what is possibly the greatest song ever created, namely "I Am a Pizza". I would highly recommend also checking out the French version, which song has taught me at least 80% of the French words I now know.
